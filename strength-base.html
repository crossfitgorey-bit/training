Phase 1 — Accumulation (Weeks 1–4)

Higher volume, moderate intensity. All main lifts at RPE 7–8. 4 sets of 5 reps. Build work capacity and ingrain technique before intensity rises. Accessory volume is high in this phase.

Monday — Squat Heavy / Bench Moderate

▾

Monday Session

Back Squat — Main Lift

4 × 5 @ RPE 8 — 3 min rest between sets

Find a weight that leaves you feeling like you had 2 reps left on the last set. If it felt easy, add 2.5–5kg. If hard, stay the same next week.

Watch Demo

Paused Squat

3 × 3 @ 70% of today's squat — 3 sec pause at bottom

Pause kills the stretch reflex — you must squat out of the hole under your own power. Exposes and strengthens weak points.

Watch Demo

Bench Press — Secondary

3 × 8 @ RPE 7 — 2 min rest

Moderate effort — this is not the main bench day. Maintain tightness in the upper back. Bar touches lower chest.

Watch Demo

Romanian Deadlift

3 × 10 @ moderate weight — 90 sec rest

Hinge at hips, soft knees, bar stays close to legs. Feel the hamstring stretch. Builds posterior chain for both squat and deadlift.

Watch Demo

Lat Pulldown or Chest-Supported Row

3 × 12 — 60 sec rest

Upper back pulling accessory. Balances the pressing volume and protects the shoulders long-term.

Watch Demo
Total session time: ~65 min. If you're short on time, drop the last accessory — never cut the main lifts short.

Wednesday — Bench Heavy / Deadlift Moderate

▾

Wednesday Session

Bench Press — Main Lift

4 × 5 @ RPE 8 — 3 min rest

Shoulder blades retracted and depressed, feet flat on floor, slight arch in lower back. The bench is a full-body lift — use leg drive.

Watch Demo

Close Grip Bench Press

3 × 8 @ 75% of today's bench

Hands shoulder-width. Builds tricep lockout strength — the most common sticking point in the bench press.

Watch Demo

Deadlift — Secondary

3 × 5 @ RPE 7 — 3 min rest

Not max effort today — this is a secondary day. Focus on set-up: hips back, lats engaged, bar over mid foot. Pull the floor apart with your feet.

Watch Demo

Tricep Pushdown (cable or band)

3 × 15 — 60 sec rest

Direct tricep work. Elbows stay tucked at sides. Full extension at the bottom.

Watch Demo

Face Pull

3 × 15 — 60 sec rest

Rear delt and external rotation work. Essential shoulder health for heavy pressers. Pull to forehead, elbows high.

Watch Demo

Friday — Deadlift Heavy / Squat Moderate

▾

Friday Session

Deadlift — Main Lift

4 × 5 @ RPE 8 — 3 min rest

Choose conventional or sumo — whichever you plan to compete or test with. Be consistent. Build to working weight over 3–4 warm-up sets.

Watch Demo

Deficit Deadlift

3 × 4 @ 70% of today's deadlift — stand on 25kg plate

Greater range of motion — strengthens the weakest part of the pull (off the floor). Improves starting position and leg drive.

Watch Demo

Back Squat — Secondary

3 × 5 @ RPE 7 — 2 min rest

Lighter than Monday. Keep the pattern consistent. These reps should feel smooth and controlled.

Watch Demo

Barbell Row

3 × 8 — 90 sec rest

Overhand grip, hinge to ~45°, row bar to lower chest. Builds upper back thickness — key for a strong deadlift lockout and bench arch.

Watch Demo

Leg Press or Leg Extension

3 × 12 — 60 sec rest

Quad accessory. Takes load off the spine while still building leg volume. Use full range of motion.

Watch Demo
Weeks 1–4: Track every working weight. You should be able to add 2.5–5kg to each main lift every week in Phase 1.

Phase 2 — Intensification (Weeks 5–8)

Volume drops, intensity rises. Main lifts move to RPE 8–9. Sets of 3 replace sets of 5. Accessories reduce by ~30%. You should feel stronger — the Phase 1 volume is paying off.

Phase 3 — Peaking (Weeks 9–12)

Volume drops to its lowest. Intensity peaks at RPE 9–9.5. Heavy singles appear in weeks 10 and 12. Week 11 is a full deload. This phase is where new 1RMs are set.

Weeks 9–10 — Building to Singles

▾

All sessions — Peaking Template

Squat / Bench / Deadlift — Warm-Up Ladder

50% × 5 → 60% × 3 → 70% × 2 → 80% × 1 → 87% × 1 → Working weight

Thorough warm-up is even more important when weights are heavy. Never rush to the working weight.

Watch Demo

Week 10 & 12 — 1RM Attempt Protocol

90% × 1 → 95% × 1 → 100%+ × 1 attempt per lift

After a made lift: add 1–2.5kg. After a miss: don't retry heavier — attempt same weight or call it. Limit max attempts to 3 per lift per day.

Watch Demo

Accessories (weeks 9–10)

1–2 light accessory movements only — 2×8 @ easy weight

Accessories exist to maintain muscle, not accumulate fatigue. Keep them brief and well within your ability in peaking weeks.

Watch Demo
🏆 Week 12: Record all 3 lifts on video. Log your total (Squat + Bench + Deadlift). This is your baseline for the next cycle — run Phase 1 again at slightly higher intensities to continue progressing.
