Skills Accelerator

Duration8 Weeks
Sessions/Week3 Sessions
Session Length30–45 Min
LevelIntermediate
When to do itBefore/After Class or Rest Day

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–2)

Establish baseline positions and movement patterns. No high-skill attempts yet — build the strength and body awareness needed for everything that follows.

Week 1

▾

Session 1 — Monday

Gymnastics — Pull & Push Foundation

Dead Hang

3 × 20–30 sec

Straight arms, active shoulders (pull them down away from ears). Build grip endurance.

Strict Pull-Up (or Ring Row)

4 × max reps (cap at 8) — 2 min rest

Full range: dead hang to chin over bar. Scale to ring rows if fewer than 3 strict pull-ups.

Hollow Body Hold

4 × 20 sec hold — 45 sec rest

Lower back pressed flat to the floor, arms overhead. The foundation of all gymnastics movement.

Arch Body Hold

4 × 20 sec hold — 45 sec rest

Lie face down, lift chest and legs simultaneously. The kip is an alternation between these two positions.

Dip Support Hold (on rings or bars)

3 × 15–20 sec

Straight arms, rings turned out, locked out at the top. Builds the upper body pressing strength needed for muscle-ups.

Rest 90 sec between exercises unless noted. Focus on quality — stop a set if form breaks down.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Handstand Foundation

Shoulder Warm-Up Circles & Banded Distractions

2 min — not for score

Prepare the shoulder joint before any overhead work. Never skip this.

Wall Walk

4 × 3 reps — 90 sec rest

Start in push-up position, walk hands toward wall as feet walk up. Go as high as comfortable. Builds overhead tolerance progressively.

Pike Push-Up

4 × 6–8 reps — 90 sec rest

Hips high, head through at the bottom. Direct pressing strength builder for HSPU.

Handstand Hold (Wall Facing)

3 × 15–20 sec — 90 sec rest

Face the wall, hands 5–6 inches from the baseboard. Keep body tight, belly button in, squeeze glutes.

If wrists get sore, rest and mobilise — do not push through wrist pain.

Session 3 — Friday

Jump Rope & Olympic Technique

Single-Under Jump Rope

3 × 60 sec continuous — 45 sec rest

Focus on wrist rotation (not big arm swings), soft knees, landing on the balls of your feet. Establish rhythm before attempting double-unders.

Snatch with PVC Pipe — Overhead Squat

4 × 5 reps — slow and controlled

Wide grip, arms locked out overhead, squat to full depth. Identify and work on any mobility restrictions.

Snatch Pull with Empty Bar

4 × 5 reps

Bar stays close to body, big hip extension at the top. No pull under yet — this is about the drive.

Use a 20kg or 15kg bar max this phase. Video yourself from the side to check bar path.

Week 2

▾

Session 1 — Monday

Gymnastics — Kip Introduction

Hollow/Arch Swing on Bar

4 × 5 controlled swings — 90 sec rest

This IS the kip. Alternate between hollow (front) and arch (back) positions. Keep arms straight throughout.

Kipping Pull-Up Attempt

4 × 3–5 reps — 2 min rest

Use the swing — as hips drive forward, pull elbows down toward hips. Do not muscle it. Stop if form is lost.

Strict Pull-Up

3 × max (cap at 6) — 2 min rest

Keep building strict strength alongside kipping — don't abandon strict work once kipping is introduced.

Hollow Body Rock

3 × 10 reps — 45 sec rest

Progress from the hold — add rocking motion while maintaining the hollow position without losing lower back contact with the floor.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Handstand — Kick-Up Practice

Wall Walk (progress from Week 1)

4 × 3 reps — go closer to the wall each rep

Target: hands 3–4 inches from wall by end of this session.

Kick-Up to Handstand (Wall)

5 attempts each side — rest as needed

Lead leg steps forward, kick back leg up, follow with second. Aim for a controlled arrival, not a crash. Partner or wall for support.

Pike Push-Up (add deficit)

4 × 6 reps — hands on plates or parallettes

Greater range of motion than flat floor. Builds deeper shoulder pressing strength.

Session 3 — Friday

Jump Rope Skill & Clean Pull

Power Jump Practice (no rope)

3 × 20 reps — fast small jumps on the spot

Simulate double-under timing without the rope. Quick wrist circles alongside. Trains the rhythm before the rope is added.

Single — Pause — Single (1-1-1 drill)

4 × 20 reps — focus on timing

Single under, brief pause jump, then attempt a double-under. Trains the height and timing needed to get two rotations.

Clean Pull with Empty Bar

5 × 3 reps

Same as snatch pull but closer grip. Focus on lat engagement keeping bar close, big jump at the top.

Weeks 1–2 check-in: Are you achieving 3 strict pull-ups? Comfortable in a wall-facing handstand for 15 sec? Consistent single-unders? If not, repeat Phase 1 before moving on.

Phase 2 — Progression (Weeks 3–4)

First real skill attempts. Bar muscle-up progressions begin, double-under attempts with rope, and barbell cycling at light load.

Week 3

▾

Session 1 — Monday

Bar Muscle-Up Progression

Kipping Pull-Up (sets of 5)

4 × 5 reps — 2 min rest

Confident and consistent before progressing to muscle-up. Hip drive must be generating the pull, not just arms.

Jumping Bar Muscle-Up (from box)

5 × 3 reps — 2 min rest

Use a box to jump and practise the transition phase — pulling elbows down and back, then pressing up. This is the hardest part of the muscle-up.

Bar Dip

4 × 6–8 reps — 90 sec rest

Full range — lower until upper arms are parallel to floor. Builds the press-out strength for the top of the muscle-up.

Goal by end of week 3: 3 jumping bar muscle-up transitions feeling smooth and confident.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Handstand — Freestanding Attempts

Handstand Hold (back to wall)

5 × 20–30 sec — 90 sec rest

Kick up with back to wall. Engage core, push floor away, spread fingers. Slightly different feel than wall-facing — prepares for freestanding.

Freestanding Kick-Up Attempts

10 attempts — rest as needed

No wall. Kick up and try to find balance. Even 1–2 seconds freestanding counts as progress. Learn to bail safely (cartwheel out).

HSPU Negative

5 × 3 reps — 3 sec lower — 2 min rest

Kick up to wall handstand, slowly lower head to floor (or ab mat) over 3 seconds. Jump back up. Builds eccentric pressing strength.

Session 3 — Friday

Double-Under Attempts & Snatch Technique

Double-Under Attempts

8 min practice — count max unbroken each attempt

Jump higher than a single-under, quick wrist rotation. If frustrating: go back to 1-1-1 drill for 2 min then retry.

Power Snatch — Barbell (40–50% 1RM)

5 × 3 reps — 90 sec rest

No squat under — receive in a partial squat. Focus on bar path (close) and hip snap. Use a light weight you can move perfectly.

Week 4

▾

Session 1 — Monday

First Bar Muscle-Up Attempts

Warm-Up Kipping Pull-Ups

3 × 5 — build rhythm

Fluid and rhythmic. No grinding. These are prep, not the main work.

Bar Muscle-Up Attempts

10–15 min practice — max 2–3 per attempt

Big hip drive, pull the bar to hips (not chin), then push chest through and press out. If not getting over: go back to jumping drill for 2 sets then retry.

Dip + Pull-Up Superset

3 rounds: 5 dips + 5 strict pull-ups — 90 sec rest

Keeps building the strength base whether muscle-up is achieved or not this week.

Not everyone gets their first bar muscle-up in Week 4 — that is completely fine. The transition strength takes time. Stay consistent and it will come.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Handstand Walking Intro & HSPU

Handstand Shoulder Taps

4 × 5 taps each side — 2 min rest

Wall handstand, shift weight to one hand and lightly tap opposite shoulder. Builds the single-arm balance needed for walking.

HSPU Negatives (3 sec lower)

4 × 4 reps — 2 min rest

Increased volume from week 3. Head touches 1 ab mat. Jump back up to top.

HSPU Attempt (from wall)

5 attempts — rest fully between

If you can do 5 strong HSPU negatives, attempt a full press-up from the floor. Use 2 ab mats under head to reduce range initially.

Session 3 — Friday

Double-Unders & Barbell Cycling

Double-Under Practice

5 × 30 sec on / 30 sec off — count max unbroken

Record your best unbroken run each session. Progress is non-linear — some days will be worse than others.

Touch-and-Go Power Clean (50% 1RM)

5 × 3 reps — fast singles with rebound off the floor

Don't reset between reps. Use the bounce — this is barbell cycling. Stay on your toes, elbows punch forward fast.

Phase 3 — Consolidation (Weeks 5–6)

Ring muscle-up progressions begin. Freestanding handstand holds target 30 seconds. HSPU from ab mats. Double-unders under 50% 1RM cycling.

Weeks 5–6 Overview

▾

Session 1 — Monday

Ring Muscle-Up Development

False Grip Ring Row

4 × 8 reps — 90 sec rest

False grip = wrist over the ring (not standard grip). Essential for ring muscle-ups. Very uncomfortable at first — build the calluses.

Ring Muscle-Up from Low Rings (feet on floor)

5 × 3 reps — 2 min rest

Use feet on floor for assistance. Focus on the transition and press-out. Reduce foot assistance over the 2 weeks.

Bar Muscle-Ups (consolidation)

3 × max reps (cap at 5)

If you have bar muscle-ups: work sets of 2–3 for quality. If not yet: continue jumping drill + bar MU attempts.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Freestanding Handstand & HSPU

Freestanding Handstand Hold

10 attempts — aim for 10+ sec each

Target: 30 sec by end of week 6. Use finger pressure to balance — lean forward, press fingertips; lean back, press heel of hand.

Strict HSPU (with ab mat)

5 × max reps — 2 min rest

1–2 ab mats under head. Full lockout at the top, controlled lower. Remove one ab mat when you can do 5 clean reps.

Session 3 — Friday

Double-Unders & Snatch Cycling

Double-Under Ladder

5-10-15-20-15-10-5 — rest 30 sec between

Sub single-unders (3:1) if needed. Track your unbroken record. Target: 20 unbroken DU by end of week 6.

Touch-and-Go Snatch (50% 1RM)

5 × 5 reps — 90 sec rest

Unbroken sets. Focus on keeping tension in the receiving position, fast elbows, controlled lower back to floor.

Phase 4 — Application (Weeks 7–8)

Put the skills to work under fatigue. Mini-WODs combining everything learned. Goal: muscle-ups in a workout, 30-sec freestanding hold, 30+ unbroken DU, confident handstand walks.

Weeks 7–8 — Skill WODs

▾

Session 1 — Monday

Gymnastics Skill WOD

EMOM 10 min — Alternate:

Odd min: 2–3 Bar or Ring Muscle-Ups / Even min: 5–8 HSPU

Scale: jumping muscle-ups + pike push-ups. Goal is consistency across all 10 rounds — choose a number you can hit every time.

Record your round-by-round performance. Any rounds where you broke or missed? That's your capacity cap for WODs.

Session 2 — Wednesday

Handstand Walk & Balance

Freestanding Handstand — Max Hold

5 attempts — record best time

Target: 30 seconds. If already there — practise deliberate stepping (handstand walking intro).

Handstand Walk (intro)

10 attempts — aim for 1–3 steps each

From a freestanding hold, deliberately shift weight and move one hand. 1 step = success. Build from there.

Session 3 — Friday

Final Skills Benchmark WOD

For Time:

50 Double-Unders → 10 Bar Muscle-Ups → 50 Double-Unders → 10 HSPU → 50 Double-Unders

Scale: 100 single-unders / jumping muscle-ups / pike push-ups. Record your time — this is your Week 8 benchmark. Compare to where you started 8 weeks ago.

🎉 Programme complete. Book a check-in with your coach to review progress and discuss what to run next.
