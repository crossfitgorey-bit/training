Phase 1 — Technique & Volume (Weeks 1–4)

All lifts at 70–80% 1RM. High rep positional drills. Back squat 3×5 building weekly. Focus: ingrain positions under moderate fatigue before intensity is added.

Session A — Snatch Focus

▾

Repeat each week of Phase 1 — adjust % per table above

Snatch Session

Snatch Warm-Up — Burgener Drill

3 rounds — PVC or empty bar

Down and finish, elbows high and outside, muscle snatch, snatch lands. Standard warm-up every session.

Snatch Pull

4 × 4 @ 95% of snatch

Bar stays close, big hip extension. Don't pull with arms — this is a leg and hip drill. Slow pull from floor to knee, then accelerate.

Snatch (full squat)

6 × 2 @ current week % — 3 min rest

Build to working weight through 3 warm-up sets. Every rep should look identical. Record a set each session for technique review.

Overhead Squat

4 × 4 @ 60% snatch — pause 2 sec at bottom

Strengthens the receiving position. Wide grip, active shoulders pressing up into the bar. Pause exposes any instability.

Phase 1 priority: positions, not weight. If a lift looks wrong on video, drop 5% and repeat.

Session B — Clean & Jerk Focus

▾

Repeat each week of Phase 1

Clean & Jerk Session

Clean Pull

4 × 4 @ 95% of clean

Same hip extension pattern as snatch pull but with clean grip. Lats engaged, bar brushes thighs, jump and shrug.

Clean & Jerk (full squat clean)

6 × 2 @ current week % — 3 min rest

Full squat receive, stand, then jerk. Each complex counts as one rep. Don't rush the jerk — reset your dip position first.

Push Press

4 × 4 @ 70% C&J

Dip drive, press overhead. Builds jerk lockout strength. Keep torso vertical during the dip — don't fold forward.

Front Squat

3 × 5 @ 80% clean

High elbows, upright torso. The front squat is what limits your clean — strengthen it directly.

Session C — Back Squat & Both Lifts

▾

Repeat each week of Phase 1

Squat & Positional Reinforcement

Back Squat

3 × 5 @ increasing weekly (see table) — 3 min rest

Full depth, bar on traps, knees tracking toes. The squat is the engine of both lifts — treat it seriously.

Snatch (moderate — 70% max)

4 × 2 — technique focus, not effort

After squatting, the lifts will feel different. This is good — trains you to lift when fatigued.

Clean & Jerk (moderate — 70% max)

4 × 1 — long rest between

Singles only on Session C. Quality over quantity. Reset fully between each attempt.

Phase 2 — Strength & Intensity (Weeks 5–8)

Lifts build to 85–92%. Heavy singles appear from week 6. Front squat intensity increases. Accessory volume reduces — quality over quantity.

Session A — Snatch (Weeks 5–8)

▾

Snatch — Intensity Block

Snatch Pull

3 × 3 @ 100% snatch (heavier than the lift)

Overloading the pull pattern. You're training the body to create more force than the snatch requires.

Snatch — Working Sets

5 × 2 @ week % — 3 min rest

Sets reduce from Phase 1 but intensity rises. Every rep at this weight should be near-maximal effort.

Heavy Single (Weeks 6–7 only)

Build to 90–92% — 1 attempt

After your working sets. Not a max effort — a heavy, confident single. If the set felt off, skip the single today.

Overhead Squat

3 × 3 @ 70% snatch

Reduced from Phase 1 but kept to maintain receiving position strength.

Session B — Clean & Jerk (Weeks 5–8)

▾

Clean & Jerk — Intensity Block

Clean Pull

3 × 3 @ 100% clean

Controlled first pull, explosive hip extension. The pull is where speed and height come from.

Clean & Jerk — Working Sets

5 × 1+1 @ week % — 3 min rest

1 clean + 1 jerk per set. Stand with the clean, regroup, then dip and drive the jerk. Don't rush the jerk after a heavy clean.

Split Jerk Technique Drill

3 × 4 @ 70% — pause in split for 2 sec

Pause in split position to check: front knee over ankle, back knee slightly bent, torso upright, arms locked. Fix any wobbles here.

Front Squat

4 × 3 @ 85% clean — 3 min rest

Heavier than Phase 1. Work the clean receiving position under real load.

Phase 3 — Peaking (Weeks 9–12)

Volume drops significantly. Lifts at 90–100%+. PR attempts in weeks 10 and 12. Full deload in week 11. This is where the hard work of phases 1 and 2 pays off.

Week 9 — Building to Heavy Singles

▾

All Sessions — Weeks 9 Template

Snatch — Warm Up to Working Weight

Warm up sets: 50% × 3, 60% × 2, 70% × 1, 80% × 1 — then 3 × 1 @ 90–95%

Each single is performed with full intent. 4 min rest between singles. The bar should feel fast — if it feels slow, stop.

Clean & Jerk — Same Protocol

3 × 1 @ 90–95% — 4 min rest

Separate session from snatch. Never attempt max snatch and max C&J in the same session during peaking.

Back Squat

2 × 2 @ 85% — keep strength topped up

Reduced volume. Just enough to maintain leg strength going into the PR weeks.

Week 10 & 12 — PR Attempts

▾

PR Day Protocol

Warm-Up (30 min)

Mobility → light singles building to 85%

Spend extra time warming up today. You want your first heavy single to feel like a medium effort. Never rush to the heavy weight.

Snatch — PR Attempt

90% → 95% → PR attempt (100%+). Max 3 attempts above 95%.

After a successful lift, add 1–2kg only. After a miss, try the same weight again. Never add weight after a miss.

Clean & Jerk — PR Attempt (separate day)

Same protocol as snatch. 90% → 95% → PR.

The jerk is often where C&J PRs are lost. Be aggressive in the dip and drive — a tentative jerk rarely succeeds at max weights.

🏆 Week 12 is the final PR day. Record all attempts on video. Book a coach to watch if possible — an external eye catches things you can't feel.
